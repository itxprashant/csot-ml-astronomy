## ML × Astronomy (Where We're Headed)

- 📄 [Dieleman, Willett, Dambre 2015 — Rotation-invariant CNNs for Galaxy Morphology (arXiv)](https://arxiv.org/abs/1503.07077) — the Kaggle Galaxy Zoo Challenge winner; see how augmentation and architecture beat a plain CNN.

- 📄 [Huertas-Company & Lanusse 2023 — Deep Learning for Galaxy Surveys (arXiv)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2210.01813) — excellent recent field survey.

- 📘 [`astroNN` — ML in astronomy in Python](https://github.com/henrysky/astroNN).

## How to Use This List

You will not read all of this. Suggested workflow for Week 3:

1. **Part 1:** watch the 3Blue1Brown convolution and backprop videos, then read the PyTorch [CIFAR-10 classifier tutorial](https://docs.pytorch.org/tutorials/beginner/blitz/cifar10_tutorial.html) — it mirrors our notebook.
2. **Build** the CNN and training loop from the starter before reading more.
3. **Part 2:** skim the scikit-learn [confusion matrix](https://scikit-learn.org/stable/modules/generated/sklearn.metrics.confusion_matrix.html) docs and one StatQuest metrics video.
4. **Astronomy:** look at the Whirlpool and the Antennae images, then classify a few galaxies on [Galaxy Zoo](https://www.zooniverse.org/projects/zookeeper/galaxy-zoo/) to feel the class ambiguity your model will face.
5. **After the week:** read [Dieleman et al. 2015](https://arxiv.org/abs/1503.07077) to see what a production-grade galaxy CNN looks like.
